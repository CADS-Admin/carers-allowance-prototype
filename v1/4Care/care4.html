Breaks in care

By break we mean any time when you spent less than 35 hours a week caring for the person you care for. For example, if:

  • they were in respite care, hospital or on holiday without you, or
  • you were in hospital or on holiday without them.

  • Have you had any breaks in caring for this person since 24 May 2013?

    This is six months before your claim date. If 'Yes', you need to provide details about these breaks

    How do breaks in care affect my claim? - Help

    Any breaks you tell us about may not affect your claim. If you get Carer's Allowance you can take breaks from caring for up to 4 weeks in every 26 weeks if you

    • have been providing 35 hours of care a week for at least 22 of the past 26 weeks
    • the person you care for has been in receipt of a qualifying benefit for that period.

    For further information, see the claim notes.

We will contact you about more breaks in care.
